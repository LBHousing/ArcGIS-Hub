What this means for renter protections

LLCs, Corporations & LPs — 46,281 units (24.8% of city)

Properties owned by these entity types that are multi-unit buildings over 15 years old are generally covered by CTPA. The single-family exemption does not apply when the owner is a corporation, REIT, or an LLC with a corporate member. These 46,281 units likely carry the strongest tenant protections available under current law.

Multi-property trusts — 21,522 units (11.5% of city)

Individual trusts with 2+ parcels control 21,522 units. Under CTPA, they're treated like individual owners — meaning single-family exemptions apply. This is the protection gap that the Block the Cuts ordinance helps address by targeting institutional-scale owners regardless of entity structure.

Single-property individuals — 118,000+ units (63%+ of city)

The majority of Long Beach housing is owned by individuals and single-property trusts. These are the homeowners and small landlords that LiBRE's advocacy explicitly protects. The Block the Cuts ordinance exempts single-family homeowners with household income under $250,000.

The bottom line: Long Beach is 60% renters. Nearly 39,000 of those rental units are controlled by multi-property owners across all entity types — operating at scale, often behind opaque ownership structures. Stronger local protections, like the Block the Cuts and Build Long Beach Ordinance, can fill the gaps that state law leaves behind.
