Why the density matters

Entities own only 7.4% of parcels but control 24.9% of all dwelling units. They disproportionately own the multi-unit buildings where most renters live. LLCs average 5.5 units per parcel. Limited partnerships average 16.3.

A policy that targets entity-owned properties reaches far more tenants per parcel than one targeting individual owners. This is why the Block the Cuts and Build Long Beach Ordinance exempts single-family homeowners with household income under $250,000 — it specifically targets institutional-scale ownership.

The pattern: Individuals lost 2,867 units. LLCs gained 1,826. In just two years, 1.54% of the city's entire housing stock shifted from individual to entity control.
