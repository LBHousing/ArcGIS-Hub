Not all trusts are created equal

The vast majority of individual trusts — 28,817 of them — own a single property. These are homeowners who set up living trusts for estate planning. LiBRE's analysis does not target these small landlords.

But 194 trusts own 5 or more parcels, controlling an average of 25 units each. These are not mom-and-pop operations. They operate at institutional scale but receive the same CTPA exemptions as a family renting out a second bedroom.

The gap: Under current law, a trust that owns 25 apartment units can claim the single-family exemption on each property — as long as they provide written notice. The same rent cap and just cause protections that automatically apply to LLC-owned buildings may not apply here, leaving thousands of renters without basic protections.
